PhD in Biodiversity Informatics

Students return in the last six months of the final year to complete and submit their theses.

Our Facilities

Our Facilities

The Laboratory of Forest Sciences and Biodiversity Informatics (LSFBI) provides state-of-the-art facilities to support advanced learning, research, and innovation. The Centre features fully air-conditioned lecture halls equipped with projectors, high-speed Wi-Fi, and an advanced computer lab designed for statistical analysis and modeling. Additionally, the LSF houses a specialized genetics lab for bioinformatics research and offers modern GPS devices and forestry instruments to aid field studies and practical training. The Centre leverages the expertise of professors and students for collaborative research, fostering a dynamic environment for scientific exploration and practical applications. Whether it's cutting-edge technology or a dedicated research team, the LSF ensures an optimal environment for education and development in forestry and related disciplines.
